# The Myth of Oedipus

The myth of Oedipus presents a profound challenge to the monomythic structure because the "hero" is also the **"shadow"** he hunts, a paradox that forces the archetypal sequence to fold in on itself. The result is a narrative cycle deformed by a **teleological trap**: the hero's every attempt to escape his journey is precisely what maps his path along it.

The *journey begins* with a displaced Supernatural Aid in the form of **Apollo's prophecy**. Unlike the canonical sequence where aid follows the hero's hesitation, the Delphic Oracle provides the "map" at the very outset. This creates an inverted Refusal of the Call through the **flight from Corinth**; Oedipus does not refuse out of cowardice, but out of a virtuous, proactive desire to protect his foster parents, making his escape the very engine of the prophecy's fulfillment.

The *Initiation phase* is characterized by a series of weak and inverted fits that highlight the **tragic and intimate nature of his enlightenment**. The Atonement with the Father is staged as a terrifying reckoning at the triple crossroads, serving as a literal patricide rather than a spiritual reconciliation. Here, the hero confronts the "father" as a Threshold Guardian to be destroyed, not an authority to be submitted to. This is followed by a subverted Woman as the Temptress stage, where Jocasta is recoded into a figure of **innocent temptation**. Her plea to "not concern yourself with prophecies" is a good-faith attempt to offer Oedipus the peace of ignorance over the ruinous pursuit of truth. Ultimately, the Ultimate Boon is reached not as a restorative elixir, but as the agonizing Self-Blinding. It is a punitive, solitary **"inner vision"** that isolates the hero from the community he has accidentally corrupted.

The *Return sequence* in Oedipus at Colonus introduces a structural repetition of the Rescue from Without. While Antigone provides the canonical support during his exile, Ismene’s arrival occurs retroactively after the hero has already crossed the return threshold of the sacred grove. This sequential divergence allows her to bring the **"news from Thebes"** that triggers the final conflict, setting into motion the hero's divinization. Ultimately, the graph tracks an inverted Freedom to Live. The hero does not achieve a serene equilibrium between the ordinary and the self-realization worlds; instead, his **Mastery of Two Worlds is denied** as Theban politics attempt to weaponize his suffering. His final transfiguration is a **freedom found only in death**, where he must vanish from the physical world entirely to become the chthonic protector of Colonus, transforming from a cursed pollutant into a divine blessing.
